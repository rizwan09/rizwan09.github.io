I am a graduate student of computer science at University of California at Los Angeles [UCLA](http://www.cs.ucla.edu/).  Before joining UCLA CS, I was a student of computer science at University of Virginia (UVA). Prior to this, I have been working as a lecturer in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) in United International University (UIU). I have received my B.Sc Engg. degree in CSE from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) in Sept, 2015. Currently I am working on two research projects: Budget Learning on Sentiment Analysis, and Language Modeling for Software Programs Synthesis in NLP@UCLA. My Adviser is Professor Kai-Wei Chang. At UVA I had been working on Modular Template Matching Approach to Visual Query Answering (VQA) in NLP LAB, UVA, and Transcending the Limits of 2D Materials Growth with Targeted Data Mining in Information Retrieval Group, UVA.   My undergrad thesis was on "An Efficient Approach to Network Voronoi Diagram in a Large Graph using Asynchronous Parallel Computation". In this thesis we developed an asynchronous parallel algorithm to find k nearest generators in a large spatial network by performing each shortest path expansion (SSSP or MSSP) at the same time and asynchronously. Our proposed scalable algorithm outperforms the existing synchronous and sequential straightforward approaches. One of my earlier research was on "A Novel Approach to Identify Spatio-Temporal Crime Pattern in Dhaka City". In this research we proposed a novel spatio-temporal street crime prediction model that exploits the historical crime data of Dhaka city to predict the possibility of a crime in a particular region at a specified time. I also built a mobile app to alert people about the crime zones. My research interest includes  Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing.
